About

I am a Sophomore at Tufts University studying Computer Science and Physics. I intend to graduate in 2018 with a triple major in Computer Science, Physics, and Mathematics. My primary areas of interest are software engineering, quantum information processing, computer graphics, computer architecture, and graphic design.

I was raised in a suburban town in the south shore of Massachusetts, small enough that my high school was a three-town regional school. During high school, I was an active member of many organizations, and helped start my school's FIRST Robotics team with friends. I graduated valedictorian of my class, and after graduation began my freshman year at Tufts University. Since then I have been studying mostly Computer Science and Physics, with significant forays into Math and Arabic.

Computer Science has always been my first academic love, and I'm interested in everything in the field. I enjoy low-level graphics work, and work that is held to tight optimization constraints &emdash; software bound to real time limits is more interesting to write. However I also enjoy front-end and even full stack web development because of the intersection of art and optimization. Though I am passionate about physics and low-level computer science work, I have no intention of downplaying the importance of art and design in my life; I can appreciate any discipline which allows that sort of creative expression. I strive to someday feel comfortable calling myself a full stack developer, and to never stop calling myself a student.

Outside of my academic and professional interests, I like to read fantasy, listen to all sorts of music, draw, and mess with hobbyist electronics. I'm also a (still-learning) speaker of Arabic and like to learn more when I have the time.
