About

I am a recent graduate of Tufts University, and majored in Computer Science, Physics, and Mathematics. The math major happened mostly accidentally, through the combined requirements of CS and Physics. I'm interested in a lot of things, among which are quantum computing, lower-level software engineering & systems programming, machine learning & data analysis, operating systems, and computer graphics. I'm currently working at Google as an SRE. Prior to that, I worked during college at State Street Global Advisors, in their Infrastructure/Architecture group, working on engineering their UNIX platforms — 20 hours a week during the semester, and full time over the summers.

Computer Science has always been my first academic love, and I'm interested in everything in the field. I enjoy low-level programming, and work that is held to tight optimization constraints — software bound to real time limits is more interesting to write. I also enjoy GUI/graphics/frontend work due to the design and art components. Over the years I've grown to really love a nice Linux environment, and would love to talk about or share my setup — my dotfiles are on GitHub, too.

In terms of physics, I'm mainly interested in quantum computing and quantum information processing; in addition to completing a BS in Physics, I enjoy keeping up with recent developments and love to talk about the field.

Outside of my academic and professional interests, I read voraciously, play and DM Dungeons and Dragons and other tabletop RPGs, listen to all sorts of music, draw, and make electronics projects when a good idea strikes me.
