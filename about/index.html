About

I am a senior at Tufts University majoring in Computer Science, Physics, and Mathematics. The math major happened mostly accidentally, through the combined requirements of CS and Physics. I'm interested in a lot of things, among which are quantum computing, lower-level software engineering, machine learning/data analysis, operating systems, and computer graphics. At this point I'm looking for jobs, and although those areas are what I would enjoy most, I like programming enough any any software engineering would be a good fit. I've been working for the past two years at State Street Global Advisors, in their Infrastructure/Architecture group, working on engineering their UNIX platforms — 20 hours a week during the semester, and full time over the summers.

Computer Science has always been my first academic love, and I'm interested in everything in the field. I enjoy low-level programming, and work that is held to tight optimization constraints — software bound to real time limits is more interesting to write. However I also enjoy front-end and even full stack web development because of the intersection of art and optimization. Over the years I've grown to really love the Linux environment, and would love to talk about or share my setup.

In terms of physics, I'm mainly interested in quantum computing and quantum information processing. I've been doing my best to learn on my own, and would love to go into the field.

Outside of my academic and professional interests, I read voraciously, play and DM Dungeons and Dragons, listen to all sorts of music, draw, and mess with hobbyist electronics.
